  <div class="pd-section-label">Overview</div>
  <div class="pd-body">
    Research into spacecraft autonomy has become increasingly important. Indeed, with a growing debris population, robots capable of performing routine tasks autonomously (such as simple repairs/maintenance, refueling, or debris removal) may become increasingly essential for actively preventing disasters. In this context, several autonomous missions to uncooperative targets (generally tumbling or orbiting Earth in an elliptical orbit) will require <span class="pd-highlight">Rendezvous and Docking operations (RPO)</span>.
    <br/><br/>
    In this project, I've worked on designing a <span class="pd-highlight">Control Scheme for a Thrust-Vectoring Satellite</span> using a <span class="pd-highlight">Koopman operator-based approach</span> for it to rendezvous with a tumbling noncooperative Satellite under Prof. <a href="https://aero.iisc.ac.in/people/debasish-ghose/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Debasish Ghose</a>. The Koopman operator provides a linear, infinite-dimensional representation of the nonlinear dynamics, enabling the use of linear control synthesis tools on an inherently nonlinear problem.
    <br/><br/>
    The framework was validated in simulation, demonstrating successful rendezvous and proximity operations with a non-cooperative target undergoing complex tumbling motion under orbital mechanics constraints.
